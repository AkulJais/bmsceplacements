Profile

Name : Ravi Sathish

Ravi Sathish was born in Bangalore, Karnataka, India. Obtained his B.E in Mechanical Engineering with 1st rank from Visveswaraya technological University in 2015. Ravi Sathish is passionate about his work. Because he loves what he does, he has a steady source of motivation that drives him to do his best. This passion led him to challenge himself daily and learn new skills that helped him to do better work. In his credit, he has eight journal publications. He has presented his research papers in person at Berlin, Germany with travel grants supported from BMSCE. He received Best Scientist Award from the Chairman of ISRO. He has been invited as a resource person for conduction of workshop on Gas turbine to faculty members of various engineering colleges.He received a grants from TEQIP-II (BMS College of Engineering) . His major contribution in the Rotatory motors of India’s first manned moon mission called Gaganyaan-1.

Why did you choose to study at BMSCE?
I chose to study at BMSCE because the Mechanical Engineering course structure and facilities really impressed me and, once I’d seen the beautiful campus, I knew this was the place for me.

What are your happiest memories of your time at BMSCE?
The elements I’ve most enjoyed during the course have been the group design projects in Year 2 and Year 3: working with like-minded people to solve sometimes very challenging problems is what engineering is all about.

Can you tell us about your work placement?
I spent my Professional Training placement year at Hindustan Aeronautics Limited (a leader in Aeronautics) as a Mechanical Engineer. I wanted a role in the field of design, which would enable me to take on my own projects. HAL offered all of this and was also close to my home in Bangalore.

What advice would you give to students hoping to work in your profession?
Work hard and follow your passion and make your dreams come true. Most importantly, be willing to learn something new everyday!

Finally, what’s your advice to students to help them make the most of their time at BMSCE?
Try to balance your studies with activities outside of course work, such as socialising and sport.
Spend time outside of contact hours reading, studying and completing assessments on-time. The more reading you do, the better placed you’ll be for future roles.
