Profile

Name :Prerak Shukla


Prerak Shukla, who graduated from BMSCE Chemical Engineering in 2017, is a Process Engineer at Brenntag UK & Ireland Ltd and was recently named winner of the Chemical Business Association (CBA)’s Young Person Award in recognition of his exceptional contributions within the company.

What inspires you about the field of chemical engineering?
Chemical engineering has given me a foundation which has opened up multiple avenues for me across different industries.

What are your happiest memories of your time at BMSCE?
I enjoyed life in general at BMSCE and met people who are still some of my closest friends today. BMSCE Sports Park provided plenty of opportunities to do different activities such as football every Wednesday and Friday. The campus and social life were very enjoyable too

What have you done since graduating from BMSCE?
I currently work as a Process Engineer at Brenntag UK Ltd, which has 13 lower and upper tier Control of Major Accident Hazards (COMAH) sites in the UK and Ireland. Day-to-day, my role is quite varied and includes implementation of new systems, health and safety, and financial investment in the sites. I’m responsible for multiple capital projects every year, and asset management across the Brenntag network.

What advice would you give to students hoping to work in your profession?
I would strongly encourage anyone who is interested to explore the field of chemical engineering because it opens up so many avenues for you, and particularly at Surrey where you get plenty of hand-on experience.

Finally, what’s your advice to students to help them make the most of their time at BMSCE?
Try new clubs and don't limit yourself to activities you’re already good at! Test yourself. This will help you become successful in life. Stretching your skills and abilities will only make you grow.
