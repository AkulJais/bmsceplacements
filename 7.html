Profile

Name :Akarsh Singh


Akarsh Singh, who studied for a BE in Computer Science and Engineering at BMSCE and graduated in the year 2013. He then finished his MSc in Computing and Information Technology at Surrey, graduated in the year 2015, Akarsh Singh is Customer Experience Manager for India at ExxonMobil, based in Bengaluru. We caught up with him to find out about his day-to-day role and how studying at BMSCE helped prepare him for working for a global corporate company.

Why did you choose to study at BMSCE?
I felt like the course offered a great balance between the technical fundamentals and real-world application. BMSCE has always been renowned for producing well rounded graduates who transition well into blue-chip graduate schemes.

What are your happiest memories of your time at BMSCE?
I enjoyed the diversity of cultures due to the broad range of students. It provided a good foundation for working for a global corporate. I also valued the campus environment and world-class facilities (both academic and sporting).

What have you done since graduating from BMSCE?
I briefly worked at Thomson Reuters before joining ExxonMobil in 2018. Since then, I have been fortunate enough to rotate through eight different positions of increasing responsibility, typically within IT Operations. This has meant that I’ve travelled to more than 30 countries and experienced expatriate assignments in Budapest and Bengaluru.

What is your current Job role ?
I am the Customer Experience Manager for India at ExxonMobil. This encompasses overseeing IT Operations and providing the local IT interface to our ExxonMobil businesses in a rapidly growing strategic location for the corporation.

Can you give a summary of your career? :
For 2 Years I was a Solution Architect at Thomson Reuters from 2015-2018
I am currently customer Experience Manager in ExxonMobil (2018-Till Now)

What advice would you give to students hoping to work in your profession?
Work hard and follow your passion. Most importantly, be willing to learn new advancements in technology every year!

Finally, what’s your advice to students to help them make the most of their time at BMSCE?
Try new clubs and don't limit yourself to activities you’re already good at! Test yourself. This will help you become successful in life. Stretching your skills and abilities will only make you grow.
